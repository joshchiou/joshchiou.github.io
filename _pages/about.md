I am a computational scientist at Eli Lilly working on proteomics for obesity clinical trials. My research focuses on translating large-scale proteomic data into mechanistic and biomarker insights to inform clinical strategy.

Previously, I was a Senior Principal Computational Geneticist in the Internal Medicine Research Unit at Pfizer, where I partnered with biologists to propose novel targets for cardiovascular and renal diseases, led cloud-native infrastructure projects for statistical genetics, and contributed to pre-competitive consortiums such as the UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project (UKB-PPP). 

I completed my PhD in Biomedical Sciences at UC San Diego in the [Gaulton Lab](https://gaultonlab.org/), where I used single-cell epigenomics to study the regulatory landscape of the pancreas and its relevance to the genetics risk of type 1 and type 2 diabetes.
