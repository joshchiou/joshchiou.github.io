I am a computational biologist at Eli Lilly working on clinical trial proteomics for obesity and cardiometabolic drug programs.
My research focuses on integrating large-scale genetic and proteomic data to identify drug targets and understand disease biology.

Previously, I was a Senior Principal Computational Geneticist at Pfizer's Internal Medicine Research Unit, where I led statistical genetics efforts in the UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project (UKB-PPP), performing pQTL analyses and protein network analyses for cardiovascular and metabolic diseases.

I completed my PhD in Biomedical Sciences at UC San Diego in the [Gaulton Lab](https://gaultonlab.org/), where I used single-cell epigenomics to study the regulatory landscape of pancreatic islets and its relevance to type 1 and type 2 diabetes risk.

**Software:**
- **[clinomics-proteomics-qc](https://github.com/EliLillyCo/clinomics-proteomics-qc)** — QC pipeline for clinical trial proteomics data (Olink, SomaScan)
- **[proteomics-accelerator](https://github.com/EliLillyCo/proteomics-accelerator)** — Multi-module Python package for clinical proteomics analysis (MMRM, pathway enrichment, bridge normalization)
